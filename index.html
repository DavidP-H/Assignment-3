Network Engineering

Responsibilities of a Network Engineer include:

  • Designing implementing new network solutions
  • Improving the efficiency of current networks
  • Installing, configuring and supporting network equipment
  • Procuring network equipment
  • Managing subcontractors involved with network installation
  • Configuring firewalls
  • Maximizing network performance through ongoing monitoring and troubleshooting
  • Investigating faults in the network

Responsibilities of a Network Engineer include:

  • Computer software/computer systems engineering
  • Computer science
  • Network security management
  • Computer systems and networks
  • Electrical/electronic engineering

What are the future possibilities?

Future possibilities in this career are very good. As society moves more towards a more online presence, with more and more people owning internet-connected devices, the need for network engineers will be in high demand to build new networks as well as expand, maintain, and upgrade existing ones.

What is the salary range?

The salary range for a network engineer in Ontario is between $51,000 and $128,000 per year, with an average network engineer in Ontario making $90,000 per year.

Why are you interested in this career?

I am interested in this career because I spend most of my time on the internet and loved the experience of learning about how my computer works and would really like to have a better understanding of how the networks that power our societies work. I am taking part in an Introduction to Networking course at Carleton University this June and am excited to find out whether this is really the career for me with some more insight into what a Network engineer might do on a day-to-day basis.

Education

Which educational institution?

I would attend Ottawa University

What are the entrance requirements?

  • English 4U or Français 4U
  • Advanced Functions 4U
  • Calculus and Vectors 4U
  • Low to mid 90s

Which first-year courses would I take?

  • ITI 1120 | Introduction to Computing I Problem solving and algorithm design. Basic principles of software engineering: structure decomposition, documentation, testing and debugging.

  • MAT 1341 | Introduction to Linear Algebra Review of complex numbers. The fundamental theorem of algebra.

  • MAT 1320 | Calculus I Intuitive definition of limits; continuity, statement of intermediate value theorem.

  • I must also minor from another Faculty and obtain 30 credits from electives.
